# Protocol: HIV Testing Services Uptake in Adolescent Girls in Sub-Saharan Africa

## Working Title
Uptake and Coverage of HIV Testing Services among Adolescent Girls in Sub-Saharan Africa: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.

## Research Question
What is the pooled prevalence of HIV testing uptake among adolescent girls (aged 15-19) and young women (aged 20-24) in Sub-Saharan Africa, and what factors influence this uptake?

## Primary Objective
To estimate the pooled proportion of adolescent girls and young women (AGYW) in Sub-Saharan Africa who have ever been tested for HIV or have tested in the last 12 months.

## Study Design
Systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) and observational studies (DHS surveys, cross-sectional studies).

## Eligibility Criteria
- **Population:** Adolescent girls (15-19) and young women (20-24) in Sub-Saharan Africa.
- **Intervention/Exposure:** HIV testing services (PITC, HBT, HIVST, etc.).
- **Comparison:** Standard care or different testing models (if applicable).
- **Outcome:** HIV testing uptake (ever tested or tested in the last 12 months).
- **Study Types:** RCTs, cross-sectional studies, DHS reports.

## Search Strategy
- ClinicalTrials.gov (registry scan)
- PubMed/MEDLINE
- WHO ICTRP
- DHS Program (Demographic and Health Surveys)

## Data Analysis Plan
- Random-effects meta-analysis of proportions.
- Subgroup analysis by:
  - Age group (15-19 vs 20-24)
  - Testing model (PITC vs HBT vs HIVST)
  - Country/Region
- Sensitivity analysis: leave-one-out.
- Publication bias: Funnel plots, Egger's test.

## Tools
- R (metafor, weightr)
- Python (data extraction)
