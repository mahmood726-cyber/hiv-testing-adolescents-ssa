In adolescent girls and young women across sub-Saharan Africa, what is the pooled uptake of HIV testing services and which delivery models perform best? We conducted a random-effects meta-analysis of seven trial arms and cohorts involving 4,410 participants to estimate the pooled proportion of those completing a test. Seven studies met eligibility criteria covering HIV self-testing, sports-based community programs, integrated clinic services, and standard-of-care models across South Africa, Zambia, Malawi, and Uganda. The pooled proportion for HIV testing was 0.68, with a 95% confidence interval of 0.51 to 0.85 and high between-study heterogeneity. Choice-based self-testing and integrated health services achieved the highest testing proportions exceeding 0.92, whereas sports-based and standard clinic models showed lower overall coverage. These findings indicate moving testing out of traditional facilities and offering self-testing choices dramatically increases reach for this priority demographic. Interpretation is limited by the small number of randomized trials, varying definitions of recent uptake, and reliance on self-reported outcomes.
